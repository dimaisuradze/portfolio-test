Selected Works

  • Lords of War

    Client: Vendome Pictures x Beside Prod. | Dir: Andrew Niccol | Role: Art Dept / Set Coordination

    The Constraint: A highly demanding international feature with heavy military props and a stretched shooting schedule forced the core art department to split across multiple locations.

    My Move: Was trusted to independently manage and coordinate secondary sets with a small team of assistants, executing complex logistics without direct supervision.

    The Result: Ensured secondary unit sets were fully prepped, dressed, and wrapped to the Production Designer's exact specifications, keeping a global production schedule intact.

  • Jonah Was Here

    Client: DeWereldVrede | Dir: Nathalie Teirlinck | Role: Art Dept / Utility

    The Constraint: Complex scheduling and unexpected departmental shortages required the art department to stretch across multiple demanding roles simultaneously.

    My Move: Acted as a utility player—bouncing between assisting the Art Director, set decorating, and fabricating custom props on-site to plug operational gaps as they appeared.

    The Result: Kept the physical production moving seamlessly, allowing the core design team to focus entirely on the director's big-picture aesthetic.

  • Vigilante

    Client: KWASSA Films | Dir: Olivier Pairoux | Role: Art Department

    The Constraint: The art department lacked a dedicated scenic painter for specific sets, risking the budget and schedule if we brought in an external contractor at the last minute.

    My Move: Stepped into the scenic painter role alongside my standard duties. I utilized existing materials to age, treat, and paint sets on the fly to match the Production Designer's vision.

    The Result: Delivered highly textured, believable, camera-ready environments that elevated the production value while strictly protecting the department's budget.

  • Single Bells

    Client: De Mensen / Streamz | Role: Assistant Art Director

    The Constraint: Maintaining massive, highly detailed festive world-building across three seasons, especially when our primary decoration supplier unexpectedly went bankrupt mid-production.

    My Move: Restructured our sourcing strategy on the fly, stretching existing stock and leveraging local alternative resources to plug the gaps without alarming the shooting crew.

    The Result: Ensured seamless visual continuity across multiple seasons, keeping the art department running smoothly without a single day of shooting delays.

  • Equal Pay Day

    Client: CZAR x Lionel Goldstein | Role: Set Design

    The Constraint: Executing highly stylized, conceptual visual storytelling on a strict, hyper-limited NGO campaign budget.

    My Move: Strategically managed and repurposed locally available resources on-site to physically build out the director's vision without relying on expensive external fabrication.

    The Result: Delivered a premium, high-impact aesthetic that met all creative requirements while strictly respecting the budget boundaries.

  • 2de Zit

    Client: Sputnik Media / Streamz | Role: Art Department

    The Constraint: Fast-paced episodic television requiring multiple rapid location turnarounds, constant prop tracking, and logistical firefighting.

    My Move: Managed critical art department logistics and rapid set dressing, ensuring the Art Director and the shooting crew always had a shoot-ready environment upon arrival.

    The Result: Reduced friction between prep and shooting, keeping the art department moving fast on a demanding episodic timeline.
